Create a reusable skill called:

ClaimSignal Claim Intelligence

Purpose:
Extract and organize insurance claim information from uploaded documents, emails, screenshots, estimates, denial letters, approval letters, supplements, photos, and notes.

The skill must convert uploaded claim materials into structured claim intelligence.

Rules:

1. Extract claim identity:
- Claim number
- Property address
- Insured/homeowner
- Carrier
- Date of loss
- Loss type
- Current status
- Full status history

2. Extract people and roles:
- Homeowner
- Adjusters
- Field adjusters
- Supervisors
- Contractors
- Vendors
- Engineers
- Ladder assist representatives

Do not assume the first name found is the insured.

3. Match documents to existing claims before creating new claims.

Compare:
- Claim number
- Address
- Carrier
- Insured name
- Adjuster names
- Email subject
- Email body
- Attachments
- Dates
- Status language

4. Resolve aliases and spelling variations.

Example:
Edwinah Bopape, Edwina Bopape, Edwina Opape, Edwina, and Eswinah Bopape are aliases of the same person only when supported by the same claim, carrier, address, document set, or timeline context.

5. Build claim timelines:
- Filed
- Inspected
- Denied
- Reopened
- Reinspected
- Approved
- Supplemented
- Paid
- Closed

6. Preserve full outcome history.

Never overwrite:
Denied → Approved → Closed

7. Identify outcome patterns:
- Denial overturned to approval
- Reinspection leading to approval
- Multi-adjuster involvement before approval
- Supplement approved
- Partial approval converted to full approval

8. Track sources for every extracted fact:
- File name
- Page number if available
- Email subject
- Screenshot name
- Upload date
- Confidence score

9. If uncertain, mark:
Needs Review

10. Special rule:
For CLM-721631 / 604 Milton Road, group all related uploaded materials under the same claim when evidence supports the connection.

- Vernon Hood
- Any other participant found in the documents

Known lifecycle:

Primary objective:
Do not just store files. Build relationships between claims, addresses, documents, emails, adjusters, carriers, timelines, and outcomes.
