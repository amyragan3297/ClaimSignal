SECTION 19

ESCALATION INTELLIGENCE ENGINE

PURPOSE

Track which escalation actions changed claim outcomes and identify the most effective escalation path for similar claims.

The system should answer:

What escalation worked before?

What should be considered next when a claim stalls, gets denied, or gets underpaid?

This must support the Playbook Engine but remain tied to real historical claim outcomes.

⸻

ESCALATION TYPES TO TRACK

* Reinspection request
* Supervisor review
* Team lead review
* Desk adjuster review
* Carrier internal escalation
* Supplement submission
* Code documentation submission
* Manufacturer documentation submission
* Repairability documentation
* Brittle test documentation
* Matching dispute documentation
* Photo evidence packet
* Estimate comparison
* Engineer report dispute
* DOI complaint
* Appraisal demand
* Attorney involvement
* Public adjuster involvement

⸻

ESCALATION RECORD

Each escalation should store:

* Claim
* Carrier
* Adjuster
* Escalation type
* Date initiated
* Reason for escalation
* Documents submitted
* Person contacted
* Response received
* Timeline impact
* Outcome before escalation
* Outcome after escalation
* Result

⸻

ESCALATION RESULT TYPES

Track results as:

* No response
* Pending
* Denial upheld
* Reinspection scheduled
* Partial approval
* Full approval
* Supplement approved
* Payment increased
* Claim reopened
* Claim closed
* Moved to appraisal
* Referred to supervisor
* Referred to legal

⸻

ESCALATION EFFECTIVENESS

Calculate only from real data:

* Total times used
* Approval rate after use
* Average days to response
* Average payment increase where available
* Carrier-specific success rate
* Adjuster-specific success rate
* Common documents that helped

If fewer than 3 comparable examples exist:

Display:

Not enough escalation history yet.

Never fabricate results.

⸻

RECOMMENDED ESCALATION PATH

When viewing a claim, suggest possible escalation paths based on similar historical claims.

Example:

Recommended Escalation Path

1. Submit organized photo evidence packet.
2. Request reinspection.
3. Cite repairability concerns.
4. Escalate to supervisor if no response within documented timeframe.

Reason:
Similar claims with this carrier and denial reason were overturned after reinspection and supporting photo documentation.

Do not present recommendations as legal advice.

Use wording:

Suggested operational next step based on historical claim patterns.

⸻

PLAYBOOK CONNECTION

If an escalation led to a successful result, allow it to be saved as a reusable playbook.

Example:

Denied -> Approved

Escalation Used:
Reinspection request + annotated photo packet

Result:
Coverage opened after review.

Reusable Strategy:
Submit slope-specific photos, repairability documentation, estimate comparison, and request reinspection in writing.

⸻

AUDIT LOGGING

Log:

* Escalation created
* Escalation updated
* Escalation result recorded
* Escalation recommendation viewed
* Escalation saved to playbook
* Escalation document attached

⸻

SECURITY AND MASKING

Preserve all existing role-based permissions.

Master Admin:
Full access.

Team Admin:
Team claim access.

Team Member:
Permitted team access only.

Individual:
Own claims only.

Executive:
Aggregate escalation trends only.

Mask claim numbers, addresses, policy numbers, phone numbers, emails, and homeowner names in aggregate intelligence views.

⸻

FINAL VALIDATION

Verify:

1. Escalations can be linked to claims.
2. Escalations can be linked to adjusters.
3. Escalations can be linked to carriers.
4. Escalation outcomes can be tracked.
5. Escalation effectiveness uses real data only.
6. Recommendations do not appear when data is insufficient.
7. Successful escalations can be saved to Playbook.
8. Audit logs capture escalation activity.
9. Role permissions are preserved.
10. Masking remains active.

SUCCESS CONDITION

ClaimSignal can identify which escalation paths worked before, connect them to claim outcomes, and suggest historically supported operational next steps without fabricating data or exposing protected claim information.
